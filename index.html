HIV

Human immunodeficieny virus

HIV causes AIDS

Symptoms include

  • mouth ulcers
  • swollen lymph nodes
  • fatigue
  • sore throat
  • muscle aches
  • night sweats
  • rash
  • chills
  • fever

HIV is aquired via fluid swapping. This includes

  • sharing needles with someone who has HIV
  • unprotected sex with someone who has HIV

HIV is spread throgh the methods described above as well as

  • perinital transmission or mother-to-child transmission
  • pre-chewed food
  • biting
  • deep open-mouth kissing
  • tattoos and body piercings

HIV's Statistics

  • More than 35,000 new infections each year
  • More than 5,000 people die each year from AIDS

Prevention

Ways to prevent the spread of HIV include

  • protecting yourself during sex
  • protecting yourself if injecting drugs
  • taking antiviral therapy ART if you have HIV, which can lower the number of copies of HIV in your blood which makes you pose virtually no risk of infecting others with the pathogen
  • prevent perinital infection by taking HIV medication, giving the baby HIV medication for 4 to 6 weeks after birth, and avoiding breastfeeding

Most recent outbreak

The most recent outbreak occurred in Seattle back in 2018 among the city's homeless population. This outbreak occurred because homeless heroin users would share needles.
